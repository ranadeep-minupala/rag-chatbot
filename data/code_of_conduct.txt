Northwind Robotics — Code of Conduct

Overview
This Code of Conduct describes the standards of behavior expected of every team member at Northwind Robotics. It applies to all team members, in every interaction connected to work — whether in writing, in video calls, in our documentation and code repositories, or at in-person gatherings. The Code is grounded in a simple idea: treat people with respect, act with integrity, and assume good intent. Questions about this Code, and any concerns about conduct, may be raised with your manager or the People team at people@northwindrobotics.com or 555-0188.

Our Values in Practice
Northwind Robotics expects team members to act with honesty, kindness, and accountability. We value transparency and default to sharing information openly. We give and receive feedback directly and respectfully. We follow through on our commitments, and when we cannot, we say so early. We assume that colleagues are acting in good faith, and we extend the same good faith to ourselves when we make mistakes — owning them, fixing them, and learning from them.

Respectful Behavior
Every team member has the right to a working environment free from harassment, discrimination, and bullying. Team members must not engage in behavior that demeans, intimidates, or excludes others. Disagreement is welcome and healthy; personal attacks are not. In a distributed, text-heavy company, tone matters: team members should write with care, give others the benefit of the doubt, and resolve misunderstandings by moving to a higher-bandwidth conversation when text is causing friction.

Anti-Harassment and Anti-Discrimination
Northwind Robotics does not tolerate harassment or discrimination on the basis of any characteristic protected by applicable law, including but not limited to race, color, ethnicity, national origin, religion, sex, gender identity or expression, sexual orientation, age, disability, marital status, pregnancy, or veteran status. Harassment includes unwelcome conduct — verbal, written, or visual — that creates an intimidating, hostile, or offensive working environment. This standard applies to interactions with colleagues, candidates, clients, partners, and anyone else encountered in the course of work.

Reporting Concerns
A team member who experiences or witnesses behavior that violates this Code is encouraged to report it. Reports may be made to the team member's manager, to any manager they trust, or directly to the People team at people@northwindrobotics.com. Reports are taken seriously, handled as confidentially as possible, and investigated fairly. Northwind prohibits retaliation against anyone who raises a concern in good faith or participates in an investigation. A team member who makes a report in good faith will not face adverse consequences for doing so, even if the concern is ultimately not substantiated.

How Concerns Are Handled
When a concern is raised, the People team will acknowledge it promptly and determine an appropriate way to look into it. Investigations are conducted fairly, giving those involved an opportunity to share their perspective. Information is shared only with those who need it to address the matter. Where a violation is found, Northwind will take appropriate corrective action, which may range from coaching and feedback to formal disciplinary measures up to and including termination, depending on the seriousness of the conduct.

Conflicts of Interest
Team members must avoid situations where personal interests conflict, or appear to conflict, with the interests of Northwind Robotics. Examples include holding a significant financial interest in a competitor, supplier, or customer; hiring or directly managing a close relative or partner; or accepting gifts of more than nominal value from parties doing business with the company. A team member who is unsure whether a situation is a conflict should disclose it to their manager or the People team, who will help determine the right course of action. Disclosure is always the safe choice.

Use of Company Resources
Team members are entrusted with company equipment, accounts, funds, and information, and are expected to use them responsibly and primarily for legitimate business purposes. Limited personal use of company equipment is acceptable within the bounds described in the Equipment and Home Office Policy. Team members must protect company and customer information and must follow the Information Security Policy. Company funds must be spent in line with the Expense Reimbursement Policy and the Travel Policy.

Confidentiality and Privacy
Team members will encounter confidential information about the company, its customers, and their colleagues. This information must be protected and used only for legitimate business purposes. Team members must not disclose confidential information to anyone who is not authorized to receive it, either during or after their employment. Personal data about colleagues, customers, and candidates must be handled in accordance with applicable privacy law and the company's privacy practices.

External Communication and Representation
When team members speak publicly in a way connected to Northwind — for example, on social media, at conferences, or in published writing — they should be honest, respectful, and clear about whether they are speaking for themselves or for the company. Only authorized spokespeople may make statements on behalf of Northwind Robotics. Team members should not share confidential company information publicly and should apply the same standards of respect online as they do internally.

Compliance With Laws
Team members are expected to comply with all laws that apply to their work, including those governing employment, data protection, anti-bribery and anti-corruption, competition, and trade. No business goal justifies breaking the law or this Code. A team member who is asked to do something they believe is unlawful or unethical should decline and raise the matter with their manager or the People team.

Acknowledgment
Every team member is expected to read, understand, and follow this Code of Conduct. Living up to the Code is a shared responsibility: it is upheld not only through formal rules, but through the everyday choices team members make to treat one another, and the people the company serves, with respect and integrity.
