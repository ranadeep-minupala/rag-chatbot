Northwind Robotics — Expense Reimbursement Policy

Overview
Northwind Robotics reimburses team members for reasonable and necessary expenses incurred in the course of their work. This policy explains which expenses are reimbursable, the limits that apply, how to submit a claim, and how reimbursements are paid. The guiding principle is simple: spend company money as if it were your own, choose the reasonable option, and keep a receipt. Questions about expenses should be directed to the People team at people@northwindrobotics.com or 555-0188.

General Principles
Reimbursable expenses are those that are directly related to performing your work, are reasonable in amount, and are supported by a valid receipt. Team members should use good judgment, avoid extravagance, and choose cost-effective options where reasonable alternatives exist. When in doubt about whether an expense is reimbursable, ask your manager or the People team before incurring the cost rather than after.

Home Office and Equipment Expenses
The one-time home office setup stipend of $1,000 and the annual home office refresh allowance of $250 are claimed through this policy by submitting receipts. As described in the Equipment and Home Office Policy, setup stipend purchases must be submitted within 90 days of purchase. These home office allowances are the mechanism for furnishing your workspace; routine office supplies such as paper, pens, or printer ink may be claimed separately as ordinary work expenses where they are genuinely needed for the role.

Software and Subscriptions
Work-related software, tools, and subscriptions are reimbursable when they are necessary for the team member's role and are not already provided by the company. Before purchasing a new tool or subscription, team members should check whether the company already provides an equivalent, and should obtain manager approval for any recurring subscription. Subscriptions billed annually should be flagged to the manager so the cost can be planned for.

Learning and Development
Northwind supports ongoing learning. Each full-time team member has an annual learning and development budget of $1,500 per calendar year, which may be used for courses, conferences, books, certifications, and similar professional development directly relevant to their role or growth at the company. Learning and development expenses require manager approval before the expense is incurred. The annual budget does not carry over and is forfeited if unused by December 31.

Travel Expenses
Expenses incurred during approved business travel — such as flights, accommodation, ground transportation, and meals — are reimbursable in accordance with the Travel Policy, which sets out booking procedures, per diem rates, and approval requirements. Where this policy and the Travel Policy both apply to a travel expense, the Travel Policy governs the specifics.

Mileage Reimbursement
When a team member uses their personal vehicle for approved business purposes, Northwind reimburses mileage at a rate of $0.67 per mile. Mileage claims must record the date, the purpose of the trip, the starting and ending locations, and the total miles driven. Mileage reimbursement covers the cost of operating the personal vehicle; it is claimed in place of, not in addition to, fuel receipts for the same trip. Commuting between a team member's home and any regular work location is not reimbursable, as Northwind is an all-remote company and team members do not have an assigned commute.

Meals and Entertainment
Meals are reimbursable when incurred during business travel or during an approved business meeting with a client, partner, or candidate. Routine day-to-day meals while working from home are not reimbursable. When claiming a business meal that includes others, the team member should record who attended and the business purpose. Alcohol is reimbursable only in moderation in the context of an approved client or team event, and team members are expected to exercise good judgment.

Expenses That Are Not Reimbursable
Some costs are not reimbursable under this policy. These include personal commuting (which does not apply at an all-remote company), personal entertainment unrelated to business, fines and penalties such as parking or traffic tickets, expenses already covered by another allowance, and any expense without an adequate receipt or business justification. If an unusual situation is not clearly addressed here, the team member should ask the People team before assuming an expense will be reimbursed.

How to Submit a Claim
Expense claims are submitted through the expense tool linked in the People portal. For each expense, the team member uploads a clear copy of the receipt and records the amount, the date, the category, and a short description of the business purpose. Claims should be submitted promptly, and in any case within 60 days of the expense being incurred. Claims submitted more than 60 days after the expense may be declined unless there is a good reason for the delay.

Approval and Payment
Submitted claims are routed to the team member's manager for approval. Managers are expected to review and approve or query claims within 5 business days. Once approved, reimbursements are paid through the regular payroll cycle, normally in the next scheduled payroll run after approval. Team members who believe a claim has been wrongly declined may raise the matter with the People team, who will review the decision.

Fraud and Misuse
Northwind trusts team members to use this policy in good faith. Deliberately submitting false or inflated claims is a serious matter and may result in disciplinary action up to and including termination, as well as recovery of any amounts wrongly paid. The company's default assumption is honesty; this provision exists only to address deliberate misuse.
