Northwind Robotics — Remote Work Policy

Overview
Northwind Robotics is an all-remote company. Every team member, including the executive team, works remotely, and no one is required to report to a central office on a daily basis. This policy describes how remote work operates at Northwind: who is eligible, how working hours and availability are handled, how we communicate, and the basic security expectations that come with working from anywhere. Questions about this policy should be directed to the People team at people@northwindrobotics.com or 555-0188.

Eligibility
All full-time team members at Northwind Robotics are remote by default from their first day. There is no application or approval process to work remotely, because remote work is the company's standard operating model rather than an exception. Team members may work from any location where they are legally authorized to work and where Northwind is able to employ them in compliance with local law. Team members who wish to relocate to a different country must notify the People team in advance, because a change of country can affect payroll, taxes, benefits, and which public holidays and statutory leaves apply.

Working Hours and Flexibility
Northwind does not require fixed working hours. Team members are trusted to structure their day around when they do their best work, provided they meet their commitments and maintain reasonable overlap with their team. Each team agrees on a set of core collaboration hours — a window during which most team members are available for synchronous discussion — and team members are expected to be reachable during those hours unless they have arranged time off. Outside core hours, team members are free to work whenever suits them. This flexibility is grounded in the expectation that team members are measured by the impact of their work rather than the hours they are visibly online.

Non-Linear Workdays
Because Northwind is asynchronous by default, team members are encouraged to take a non-linear approach to their day. A non-linear workday means that work does not have to happen in one continuous block; a team member might work in the morning, take several hours in the afternoon for personal commitments, and return to work in the evening. This is fully acceptable as long as commitments are met and the team member remains reachable during agreed core hours. Managers should not treat a non-linear schedule as a performance concern.

Availability and Time Off
When a team member is unavailable during their normal working pattern, they are responsible for communicating that clearly in advance through their team's agreed channels and by updating their status. For absences of a day or more, time off should be requested through the People portal as described in the Leave and Time Off Policy. Team members are not expected to monitor messages or respond to requests while on approved time off, on a public holiday, or during a company-wide closure.

Communication and Documentation
Remote work at Northwind depends on writing things down. The company follows a handbook-first approach: processes, decisions, and policies are documented in a single source of truth so that any team member can find an answer without interrupting a colleague. Team members are expected to favor written, asynchronous communication over real-time meetings wherever practical, to record the outcomes of meetings in writing, and to default to sharing information openly rather than restricting it to a need-to-know basis. The detailed norms for channels, response times, and meetings are described in the Communication Policy.

Home Working Environment
Team members are responsible for maintaining a home working environment that allows them to work safely and productively, including a reliable internet connection. Northwind supports team members in setting up their workspace through the equipment stipend and provisioning described in the Equipment and Home Office Policy. Team members who travel or work from temporary locations remain responsible for ensuring they have adequate connectivity and a suitable environment for confidential work.

Information Security Expectations
Working from anywhere carries security responsibilities. Team members must keep their company devices locked when unattended, use the company-provided password manager and multi-factor authentication for all work accounts, and avoid conducting confidential work on public or unsecured networks without the company VPN. Confidential documents must not be left visible on screens in public spaces. Any lost or stolen device, or any suspected security incident, must be reported to the Security team immediately at security@northwindrobotics.com. Detailed security requirements are set out in the Information Security Policy.

In-Person Gatherings
Although Northwind is all-remote, the company recognizes the value of occasional in-person connection. Northwind organizes optional team and company gatherings during the year, and participation is never mandatory. Travel for approved company gatherings is arranged and reimbursed according to the Travel Policy. Team members who are unable or prefer not to attend in-person events are fully included in the alternatives provided.

Equipment and Expenses
Northwind provides the equipment team members need to work effectively and reimburses reasonable work-related expenses. The specifics — including the home office stipend, how hardware is provisioned and returned, and what counts as a reimbursable expense — are covered in the Equipment and Home Office Policy and the Expense Reimbursement Policy. Where this policy and those policies overlap, the more specific policy governs.

Responsibilities of Managers
Managers of remote teams are responsible for setting clear expectations, agreeing core collaboration hours with their team, documenting decisions, and evaluating team members on outcomes rather than visible activity. Managers are expected to be deliberate about including every team member regardless of time zone, to make meetings optional wherever possible by sharing agendas and notes in advance, and to model the same healthy boundaries around availability and time off that the company asks of all team members.
