Northwind Robotics — Information Security Policy

Overview
Working from anywhere gives Northwind Robotics team members enormous flexibility, and it also means that information security depends on the daily choices each team member makes. This policy describes the security practices every team member must follow to protect company, customer, and colleague information. It is written to be practical rather than burdensome: most of it is common-sense habits applied consistently. Questions about security, and all security incidents, should be directed to the Security team at security@northwindrobotics.com. General questions may also be directed to the People team at people@northwindrobotics.com or 555-0188.

Shared Responsibility
Security at an all-remote company is a shared responsibility. The Security team provides tools, guidance, and monitoring, but the protection of information depends on every team member following good practices wherever they work. Team members are expected to take security seriously, to ask the Security team when they are unsure, and to report anything suspicious promptly. A culture where people feel safe reporting mistakes quickly is far more secure than one where mistakes are hidden, so the company treats good-faith reports as exactly the right thing to do.

Accounts and Passwords
Every team member must use the company-provided password manager to create and store strong, unique passwords for all work accounts. Passwords must not be reused across accounts and must not be shared with anyone, including colleagues. Multi-factor authentication must be enabled on every work account that supports it. Team members must never approve a multi-factor authentication prompt they did not initiate, and should report any unexpected prompt to the Security team, as it may indicate an attempted compromise.

Device Security
Company devices must be protected with full-disk encryption, a strong device password or passcode, and an automatic lock that engages when the device is unattended. Team members must keep the operating system and security software up to date, and must not disable the security tools the company installs. Unapproved software must not be installed on company devices. Company devices must be locked whenever they are left unattended, even briefly, and especially in public spaces.

Protecting Confidential Information
Confidential company and customer information must be accessed and stored only through approved company systems. Team members should not copy confidential information to personal devices, personal cloud storage, or personal email. When working in a public space, team members must be aware of who can see their screen and must not leave confidential information visible. Confidential documents should be shared only with people who are authorized to see them, using the company's approved sharing tools and the principle of least access — granting the minimum access necessary.

Working on Networks
Team members may work from home networks, offices, cafes, and other locations. On any network that is not trusted — such as public Wi-Fi in a cafe, airport, or hotel — team members must use the company VPN before conducting confidential work. Home networks should be secured with a strong router password and current firmware. Team members should avoid conducting sensitive work on networks they do not control unless the VPN is active.

Phishing and Social Engineering
Phishing and social engineering are among the most common security threats. Team members should be cautious with unexpected messages that create urgency, ask for credentials, request unusual payments, or contain unexpected links or attachments. Team members must never enter their work credentials into a site reached through a link in an unsolicited message, and should verify unusual requests through a known, separate channel. Suspected phishing should be reported to the Security team at security@northwindrobotics.com, even if the team member is not sure.

Handling Personal Data
Personal data about colleagues, customers, and candidates must be handled in accordance with applicable privacy law and collected and used only for legitimate purposes. Team members should access personal data only when they need it for their work, store it only in approved systems, and never share it with unauthorized parties. Questions about handling personal data should be directed to the Security team or the People team.

Reporting Incidents
Any lost or stolen device, any suspected account compromise, any accidental disclosure of confidential information, and any other suspected security incident must be reported to the Security team at security@northwindrobotics.com immediately. Fast reporting allows the team to revoke access, secure devices remotely, and limit harm. Team members will not be penalized for reporting a genuine mistake in good faith; the priority is always to contain the issue quickly. When in doubt about whether something is worth reporting, report it.

Leaving the Company
When a team member leaves Northwind Robotics, their access to company systems is revoked as part of offboarding, and all company equipment is returned as described in the Equipment and Home Office Policy. Team members' obligations to protect confidential information continue after their employment ends. Team members must not retain copies of confidential company or customer information after leaving.
