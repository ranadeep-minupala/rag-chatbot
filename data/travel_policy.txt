Northwind Robotics — Travel Policy

Overview
Although Northwind Robotics is an all-remote company and most work happens without travel, team members occasionally travel for approved company gatherings, client or partner meetings, conferences, and similar business purposes. This policy explains when travel is appropriate, how to get approval, how to book travel, the per diem rates for meals and incidentals, and how travel expenses are reimbursed. Questions about travel should be directed to the People team at people@northwindrobotics.com or 555-0188.

When Business Travel Is Appropriate
Business travel should be purposeful. Because Northwind is built for remote, asynchronous work, travel is reserved for occasions where being in person adds clear value — for example, a company or team gathering, an important client engagement, a conference relevant to the team member's role, or onboarding events. Team members should consider whether the goal of a trip could be achieved remotely before requesting travel, and should weigh the cost and the environmental impact against the benefit.

Approval
All business travel must be approved by the team member's manager before any booking is made. For travel expected to exceed a significant cost threshold, or for international travel, additional approval from the department lead may be required; the People team can confirm whether a particular trip needs that extra sign-off. Approval should be obtained well in advance so that travel can be booked at reasonable cost. Travel booked without prior approval may not be reimbursed.

Booking Travel
Once travel is approved, team members book their own flights, accommodation, and ground transportation through the company travel tool linked in the People portal, or directly where the travel tool does not cover a particular need. Team members should book in economy class for flights, choose mid-range accommodation that is safe and conveniently located, and select cost-effective ground transportation. Where a team member has a genuine need for an exception — for example, an accessibility requirement or an unusually long flight — they should discuss it with their manager and the People team in advance.

Flights
Flights should be booked in economy class. Team members are encouraged to book in advance to secure reasonable fares and to choose sensible routings rather than the most expensive direct option when a reasonable alternative exists. Northwind does not require team members to take inconvenient routings purely to save small amounts; the standard is reasonableness, not minimization at the expense of well-being. Any frequent-flyer benefits a team member earns on business trips belong to the team member.

Accommodation
Team members should book clean, safe, mid-range accommodation that is reasonably close to the location of the business activity. Northwind does not set a single global nightly cap, because reasonable accommodation costs vary widely by city; instead, team members are expected to choose sensibly for the location, and the People team can advise on what is reasonable for a particular city if needed. Incidental accommodation costs such as in-room internet required for work are reimbursable.

Per Diem for Meals and Incidentals
For days of business travel, Northwind provides a per diem to cover meals and incidental expenses, so that team members do not need to retain individual meal receipts. The standard per diem is $60 per full day of travel for domestic trips and $75 per full day for international trips. The per diem is prorated for partial travel days, with a half-day rate applied to the day of departure and the day of return. Where a meal is already provided — for example, meals included at a conference or company gathering — the per diem is reduced accordingly for that day. Team members who prefer to claim actual meal costs with receipts instead of the per diem may do so, but should choose one method consistently for a given trip.

Ground Transportation
Reasonable ground transportation — airport transfers, taxis or rideshare, public transport, and short-term car rental where needed — is reimbursable. When a team member uses their personal vehicle for an approved business trip, mileage is reimbursed at the standard rate of $0.67 per mile as described in the Expense Reimbursement Policy. Parking and tolls incurred during business travel are reimbursable; fines and penalties such as parking tickets are not.

Company and Team Gatherings
Travel for approved company-wide gatherings and team offsites is arranged with support from the People team, and the associated travel and accommodation are reimbursed under this policy. Attendance at in-person gatherings is optional, consistent with the Remote Work Policy, and team members who do not attend are fully included through remote alternatives. Team members with accessibility needs related to travel should contact the People team in advance so arrangements can be made.

Travel Safety and Insurance
Team members are covered by company travel arrangements while on approved business travel. Team members should share their itinerary with their manager for any significant trip, keep emergency contact information accessible, and follow any location-specific guidance the company provides. If a team member feels unsafe during business travel, they should prioritize their safety and contact the People team as soon as practical; the company will support reasonable decisions made in the interest of personal safety.

Submitting Travel Expenses
Travel expenses are claimed through the expense tool in the same way as other expenses, following the Expense Reimbursement Policy. Receipts are required for flights, accommodation, ground transportation, and any actual meal costs claimed instead of the per diem; the per diem itself does not require individual meal receipts. Travel claims should be submitted within 60 days of the trip, consistent with the general expense claim deadline.
