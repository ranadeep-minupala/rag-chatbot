Northwind Robotics — Benefits Overview

Overview
Northwind Robotics offers a range of benefits designed to support the health, financial security, and well-being of team members and their families. Because Northwind employs people in many countries, the exact benefits a team member receives depend on their country of employment and local law; this document describes the company's overall approach and the benefits that apply broadly. For the specific details of your benefits package, contact the People team at people@northwindrobotics.com or 555-0188.

Our Approach to Benefits
Northwind aims to provide competitive, meaningful benefits that work for a distributed, global team. Where possible, the company seeks to offer comparable support to team members regardless of where they live, while respecting the differences in healthcare systems, retirement structures, and statutory requirements across countries. The People team reviews benefits regularly and communicates any changes through the People portal.

Health Coverage
Northwind provides health coverage appropriate to each team member's country of employment. In countries where private health insurance is customary, the company offers a health plan with company contributions toward premiums for the team member and, in many locations, their dependents. In countries with comprehensive public healthcare, Northwind provides supplementary coverage or an equivalent benefit where appropriate. Details of the health plan available in a team member's location, including what is covered and how to enroll, are provided by the People team during onboarding.

Retirement and Savings
Northwind supports team members in saving for the future through a retirement or long-term savings benefit appropriate to their country. In locations with employer-sponsored retirement plans, the company contributes to the plan according to the terms communicated to the team member. In locations where such plans are not customary, Northwind provides an equivalent savings benefit where it is able to do so. The specific structure and any company contribution are confirmed by the People team based on the team member's location.

Wellness Stipend
Every full-time team member receives an annual wellness stipend of $600 per calendar year to spend on activities and resources that support their physical and mental well-being — for example, gym memberships, fitness equipment, mental health support, or wellness apps. The wellness stipend is claimed through the expense process described in the Expense Reimbursement Policy by submitting receipts. The wellness stipend does not carry over and is forfeited if unused by December 31.

Mental Health Support
Northwind recognizes that mental health is as important as physical health, especially in a remote environment. The company provides access to confidential mental health support resources, the details of which are available in the People portal and from the People team. Team members are encouraged to use their sick leave for mental health needs in the same way they would for physical illness, and managers are expected to treat mental health with the same respect and confidentiality as any other health matter.

Time Off and Leave
Paid time off, sick leave, parental leave, and other leave benefits are an important part of the overall benefits package. These are described in full in the Leave and Time Off Policy, which sets out the 25 days of annual PTO, 10 days of paid sick leave, 16 weeks of fully paid parental leave, and the various statutory leaves available to team members. Public holidays, floating holidays, and company-wide closures are described in the Holidays and Office Closure Policy.

Learning and Development
Northwind invests in the growth of its team members. As described in the Expense Reimbursement Policy, each full-time team member has an annual learning and development budget of $1,500 for courses, conferences, books, and certifications relevant to their role and growth. Team members are encouraged to use this benefit and to discuss their development goals with their manager.

Home Office Support
Northwind supports team members in working comfortably from home through the one-time home office setup stipend of $1,000 and the annual home office refresh allowance of $250, described in the Equipment and Home Office Policy. Together with company-provided equipment, these benefits help team members create a safe, ergonomic, and productive workspace.

Eligibility and Enrollment
Most benefits described here are available to full-time team members. Part-time team members may be eligible for certain benefits on a prorated basis, and contract team members are governed by their individual agreements. Enrollment in applicable benefits happens during onboarding, and the People team guides each new team member through the options available in their location. Team members can review and update their benefit elections, where applicable, through the People portal, and should contact the People team with any questions.
